The process of Transcription is somewhat similar to the process of DNA replication in that the double stranded DNA is first separated by a Helicase. Then one of the two possible strands of DNA is copied by a polymerase enzyme. When complete, the DNA helix is restored after having copied a gene from the DNA molecule.

There are a couple of important differences though. The enzyme used in Transcription to copy from the DNA is called RNA Polymerase since we are now making an RNA strand, not a DNA one. The RNA polymerase is making a single stranded copy of the DNA but it is making this single strand out of RNA. The resulting RNA strand is called a Messenger RNA or mRNA.

Another difference seen here is the introduction of a 5th base. When the RNA polymerase sees an A on the DNA strand it matches up a different nucleotide. Instead of the normal T that would be inserted the polymerase inserts a U (or uracil) nucleotide. There are no T’s in a messenger RNA strand; U’s are used instead.
