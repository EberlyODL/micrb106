All living cells have minimal requirements they absolutely must. These essential elements include:

  • Carbon
  • Hydrogen
  • Nitrogen
  • Oxygen
  • Phosphorous
  • Sulfur

Living cells require both an energy source and a source of carbon to build the necessary components for all cell structures.

Carbon can come from one of two sources, either the breakdown of organic material or from the air.

  • A Heterotroph gets organic carbon from the breakdown of other organic matter.
  • An Autotroph takes CO2 from the air, keeps the Carbon and releases the Oxygen.

Energy is acquired from one of two sources:

  • A Phototroph gathers light energy from the sun using chlorophyll.
  • A Chemotroph gets energy through the breakdown of organic molecules like sugar.

These terms are mixed together in 4 ways to describe the characteristics of living organisms. For example, a plant would be a PhotoAutotroph, they take energy from the sun and Carbon from CO2 in the air. You are a ChemoHeterotroph. Microorganisms can be found as examples in every group.
