Causing Disease:

The symptoms of the disease state are caused primarily by the damage that pathogens do once they become established and reproduce to sufficient numbers. Until this time, the person may have no idea that anything is going on. By the onset of symptoms, the damage being done is already underway. 

The damage caused by a particular pathogen varies and some cause damage in multiple ways. Pathogens secrete a variety of extracellular enzymes that may cause cell death (as in the case of gangrene) or merely inflammation (as with a rash). Some examples include:

  • Hyaluronidase - degrades a main component of connective tissue
  • Collagenase - degrades collagen also found in connective tissue
  • Coagulase - causes clotting of blood plasma fibrin, used to stop bleeding, thus depleting it
  • Fibrinolysin - degrades fibrin
  • Hemolysin - destroys red blood cells

Pathogens may secrete exotoxins and endotoxins which have wide ranging effects. Many of these effects alter body physiology as in tetanus, botulism and cholera. We'll talk more about these toxins next.
