Function of Capillaries

Capillaries continuously leak plasma into the surrounding tissues. Left uncollected this excess interstitial fluid would build up and make the tissue highly swollen.

Specialized capillaries collect this excess fluid and return it to the bloodstream. Once the interstitial fluid enters these vessels, it becomes Lymph. The capillaries that collect this fluid are called Lymphatic vessels. Lymphatic vessels are similar to veins in the way the fluid passes through them.
