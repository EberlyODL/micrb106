Two other terms are useful when discussing the control of organisms. Bacteriocidal agents will actually kill vegetative cells and destroy spores. Bacteriostatic agents will only prevent or slow the growth of organisms. As a general rule, Disinfectants tend to be Bacteriocidal and Antiseptics tend to be Bacteriostatic.

The overall effectiveness of any method used to control microbes depends on a number of factors:

  • The number or bacteria present greatly impact the effectiveness of the method used. In general, the more microbes the harder it is to control them.
  • Environmental factors such as temperature or pH of the liquid can also affect how well a particular method works.
  • The dosage or concentration of the agent used will alter effectiveness. Diluted bleach won't work as quickly as a concentrated amount would.
  • Sometimes the presence of inhibitory matter, like organic material will affect the method in use.
  • Lastly, some organisms are simply harder to destroy than others. For example endospores are much harder to destroy than vetetative cells.

As you can see, even the cell wall structure of bacteria can contribute to resistance to control methods. Gram positive cells are less resistant that gram negative cells. Some organisms like Mycobacterium tuberculosis are particularly hard to eradicate.
