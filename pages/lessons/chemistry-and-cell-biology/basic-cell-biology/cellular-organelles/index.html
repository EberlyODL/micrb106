As discussed earlier, Eucaryotic cells are complex cells that make up plants and animals. These types of cells have a number of specialized internal structures that perform specific jobs for the cell. These organelles or "little organs" are found in the cytoplasm of all cells.

Specialized Structures

Additionally, some cells have even more specialized structures that project outside of the cell itself.

  • Nucleus: This is where the cell stores it's genetic information in the chromosomes.

  • Ribosomes: These are used to make proteins.

  • Endoplasmic reticulum: This comes in two forms; smooth and rough. Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER) has ribosomes on it's surface, and Smooth ER does not.

  • Mitochondria: These structures convert sugar into ATP which is used anytime the cell requires energy.

  • Golgi: This is an organelle that sorts proteins produced by ribosomes and delivers them to their final destination in the cell.

  • Cilia: These are found on the surface of some cells. They can move back and forth and push substances across the surface of the cell.

  • Flagella are used to propel cells from one place to another. Sperm cells use these to move around.
