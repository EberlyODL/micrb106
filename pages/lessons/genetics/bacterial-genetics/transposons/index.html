These transposable elements are genes that have the ability to move or hop around from one place to another within a chromosome. Sometimes they can jump from a chromosome to a plasmid. Then they would be able to be transferred to another cell as part of that plasmid. Somtimes the transposon replicates itself before it moves, other times it does not. 

These genes can encode for a variety of features including antigenic characteristics, colony morphology or color and most importantly, antibiotic resistance.
