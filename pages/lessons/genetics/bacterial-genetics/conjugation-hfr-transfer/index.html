Sometimes the Fertility plasmid inserts itself into the bacterial chromosome. It becomes a recombinant. When this happens it's called an Hfr (high frequency recombinant). 

Normally the F plasmid will insert itself into the chromosome for a time. Then it extracts itself to transfer a copy to another F- cell and then reinserts into the chromosome. This can happen many times.

Occasionally though, when the plasmid extracts itself from the chromosome it takes a bit of the bacterial chromosome with it. Then when it passes a copy to another F- cell, the cell not only gets the F plasmid it also gets a bit of DNA from the first cells as well. 

Again, like transduction, some DNA from one bacterium is accidentally transferred to another bacterium.
