Bt toxin is produced by the bacterium Bacillus thuringensis. The toxin attacks and destroys insects, particularly caterpillars. The insects ingest the toxin and it effectively kills them. This insecticide has been used for decades and has never been shown to cause problems in animals. Even studies looking at possible allergic reactions on humans have not been able to find any cause for concern. So how does Bt kill insects then?

 

Mode of Action of Bt Toxin
 
 
Step 1: Insects ingest Bt toxin
 

Step 2: The toxin binds to receptors on the gut and cause the insect to stop eating.
 
 
Step 3: The toxin then breaks open the lining of the gut and the toxin gets into other tissues.
 
 

Step 4: The insect dies.
